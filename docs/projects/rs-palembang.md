## Overview

Analyzed land cover change around the Jakabaring toll gate in Seberang Ulu, Palembang, to assess how Trans Sumatera Toll Road development is driving the area's growth in line with the city government's development plan.

**Study Area:** Seberang Ulu (Jakabaring Toll Gate area), Palembang — 85.642 km²

---

## Methods & Tools

**Data Sources**

- Sentinel-2 Level 1C imagery, 2017, 2018, and 2019

**Processing Steps**

1. Calculated the Normalized Difference Built-up Index (NDBI) from Sentinel-2 imagery to map built-up areas.
2. Classified NDBI results into four density classes (non built-up, low, medium, high density built-up), then simplified into two main classes: non built-up and built-up.
3. Compared built-up extent across 2017–2019 to analyze the toll road's impact on development growth.

**Tools Used**

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Sentinel-2 imagery | Source data for NDBI analysis (2017–2019) |
| NDBI (Normalized Difference Built-up Index) | Mapping and classifying built-up areas |
| Web GIS | Publishing interactive results |

---
## Key Findings

- Built-up land in Seberang Ulu grew by 6.15% between 2017 and 2019.
- Growth aligns with the Palembang city government's plan to intensify development in the Seberang Ulu area following Trans Sumatera Toll Road construction.
